9/9/23 - This week in engineering was very fun. I learned how to use tinkercad (the basics) and started making a rube goldberg machine. We began constructing our rube goldberg machine in tinkercad. I shared the cad with my group and we got to designing. We started with making a lever which launches a ball with the force of a baseball. The ball then goes to an inclined plaine, sliding into a cylinder. The cylinder rolls onto another inclined plaine then collides with dominos. The dominoes then fall into a pulley which rises another ball into yet another inclined plaine.Finally, the ball rolls into a needle which pops the balloon. The machines that we will be using are inclined plains, a lever, and a pulley. I think the most challenging part of this project will be the ending. 10/6/23 - This week we went over bioengineering slides. We learned that bioengineering is the application of biology and the tools of engineering to create useable, tangible, and econmically-viable products. The medical uses for bioengineering include prosthetics such as arms, legs, and even hearts that can work for 30 minutes. We also learned about biomimicry. Biomimicry is the study of mimicking biology and living orgnaisms to benefit our devices. For example, gekkos have velcro like feet that allow them to stick onto surfaces with ease. We can mimic this and use it for mars rovers. We also learned about the mantis shrimp which has the fastest and hardest punch. It even creates a flash of light when it hits something. After we went over bioengineering, we had to build a prosthetic hand with cardboard, rubber bands, string, and tape. My partner and I started with an idea which had 3 fingers and 3 rings. We stuck a finger in each ring and the prosthetic moved with it. After we realized that that wouldnt grip onto anytihng, we switched the design. We switched by removing the rings off of the hand and putting rope on each finger. I tied all of the ropes together and with a simple pull, all of the fingers would move. This design worked way better than the previous one and was able to pick up an empty water bottle. If I could do something different, If I had more time and was able to improve the hand, I would add more fingers just to get an overall better grip around the bottle. 10/13/23 - This week we learned about aerospace engineering. Aerospace engineering is a branch of engineering that involves design, manufacturing, and science of aircraft spacecraft. It involes strucutral design, navigation and control, guidence, and instrumentation. The two branches are aeronautical engineering and astronautical engineering. Aeronautical engineering involves crafts that stay in earths atmosphere. Astronautical engineering involves crafts that operate in outer space. The four forces on a flying object are weight, thrust, lift, and drag. Weight lowers it, thrust brings it forward, lift rises it, and drag brings it back. This week we also looked at quiz analytics.
