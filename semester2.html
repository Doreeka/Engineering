1/19/2024 - This week we went over chemical engineering. Chemical engineering is the branch of engineering that deals with chemical production and the manufacture of products through chemical processes. A day or two after learning about chemical engineering, we made oobleck. Oobleck is a non-utonium liquid, meaning that it is solid when force is applied, and liquid when it is still. We made it by putting corn starch in a bowl then slowly adding water and mixing until it became the consistency we desired. I had lots of fun making and playing with oobleck, and I would recomend it to anyone.

2/26/2024 - This week we fullty completed, tested, and competed with our cars.

  1. Our car worked much better than my partner and I thought it would. It had a simple cardboard base with a mouse trap on it. Connected to the mouse trap was a rod with a string. The string was attatched to the back axle, which was made up of a straw and skewers. When the mouse trap would go off. the rod would slowly get pulled and pull the rope with it. When the rope got pulled. the axle rotated and the car would start moving.
  2. The furthest distance of our first car was 76.5 feet, and the furthest distance of our second car was 104 feet.
  3. The main differences between our two cars were the wheel stability and the rod length. These two factors helped the car go farther beause without straight wheels, we would have to stop it and adjust it. With a longer rod, the car could pull farther. The second car performed better because it had straighter wheels and a longer rod.
  4. If we were to make a 3rd car, there is only one thing that I would change. I would make the rod longer to get more distance. The rod can extend how far the car travels. I know this becuase our 2nd car with a longer rod went farther than our 1st car with a shorter rod. I learned that the car can go farther with a longer rod.
