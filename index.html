9/9/23 - This week in engineering was very fun. I learned how to use tinkercad (the basics) and started making a rube goldberg machine. We began constructing our rube goldberg machine in tinkercad. I shared the cad with my group and we got to designing. We started with making a lever which launches a ball with the force of a baseball. The ball then goes to an inclined plaine, sliding into a cylinder. The cylinder rolls onto another inclined plaine then collides with dominos. The dominoes then fall into a pulley which rises another ball into yet another inclined plaine.Finally, the ball rolls into a needle which pops the balloon. The machines that we will be using are inclined plains, a lever, and a pulley. I think the most challenging part of this project will be the ending. I think that making the needle hit the balloon will be a difficult task since we can't hold the needle completely still.

9/15/23 - This week, we completely finished our rube goldberg machine. The hardest part of it was making the begining and end consistent. The begining was hard because we had to find the sweet spot to drop the ball every time. The ending was the hardest part, though. The cup kept on messing up no matter what we did. That was until Mr. Poole gave us a brilliant idea to tie two strings to the cup instead of one. This gave the cup stability while going up which kept it straight enough to pop the balloon at the end. We could have made it better by making it more consistent. This could be done by making the cuts straighter This video is our final result which took many many tries.
